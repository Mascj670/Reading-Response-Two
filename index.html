My website is a shifting house next to a river of knowledge. What could yours be?
by Laurel Schwulst

Quotes and Questions

Quote
“A website is both subject and object at once. In other words, a website creator becomes both author and architect simultaneously.”
Question
How does the dual role of being both the author and architect influence the design and narrative of a website?
Quote
“Today more than ever, we need individuals rather than corporations to guide the web’s future. The web is called the web because its vitality depends on just that—an interconnected web of individual nodes breathing life into a vast network.”
Question
How can individual creators ensure the web remains a space for genuine expression and not just commercial interests?
Quote
“Artists excel at creating worlds. They do this first for themselves and then, when they share their work, for others. Websites allow the author to create not only works but also the world.”
Question
In what ways can artists leverage the digital space of websites to enhance their creative expression and world-building?
Quote
“A website can be anything. It doesn’t (and probably shouldn’t) be an archive of your complete works. Websites are living, temporal spaces.”
Question
How can the evolving nature of websites be used to reflect the dynamic nature of an artist's or creator's journey?

Reading Response

The digital realm, as explored in "My website is a shifting house next to a river of knowledge," offers a unique canvas for creators. The duality of a website being both a subject and an object emphasizes the intricate relationship between its form and function. As both the storyteller and the builder of the story's environment, a website creator has the power to shape the narrative and the space it inhabits. This dual role allows for a harmonious blend of design aesthetics and content, ensuring that the website remains a genuine reflection of the creator's intent. Furthermore, the article underscores the importance of individuality in shaping the web's future. In an era dominated by corporate interests, the vitality of the web hinges on the contributions of individual creators. Their genuine expressions breathe life into the vast network, ensuring that the web remains a space for authentic voices and not just commercial agendas. Artists, with their innate ability to create worlds, find in websites a platform that amplifies their creative expression. Websites, being living and temporal spaces, evolve with the artist's journey, reflecting their growth, experiences, and emotions. They are not just archives but dynamic digital canvases that capture the essence of the artist's world. In conclusion, the article beautifully encapsulates the poetic potential of websites. In the hands of genuine creators, websites transcend their digital confines, becoming living embodiments of ideas, emotions, and narratives.
